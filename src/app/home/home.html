Welcome to the Gym Leaders

Kanto Region

Kanto is the foundational region of the Pokémon world, first appearing in the original Pokémon Red and Blue games. Inspired by the real-life Kantō region of Japan, it is characterized by its temperate climate and a mix of high-tech urban centers like Saffron City and quiet, rural areas like Pallet Town. As the first region ever introduced, it debuted the original 151 Pokémon and features the iconic Indigo League. Trainers traveling through Kanto must challenge legendary Gym Leaders such as Brock, Misty, and Giovanni to earn badges, eventually making their way to the Indigo Plateau to face the Elite Four.

Johto Region

The Johto region was introduced in the second generation of games, Pokémon Gold and Silver, and is located directly west of Kanto. This region is deeply rooted in history and tradition, with a culture heavily inspired by Japan's Kansai region. It is famous for its ancient architecture, such as the Bell Tower and Burned Tower in Ecruteak City, which are tied to the myths of the legendary Pokémon Ho-Oh and Lugia. Johto added 100 new species to the Pokédex and introduced significant gameplay mechanics like the day-and-night cycle and the discovery of Pokémon Eggs.
